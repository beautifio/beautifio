## 1. FOOTBALL PLAYER (Pemain Sepak Bola Profesional)
**180 lines · ~1361 words · ⚠️ Highest word count — length is a strength but also reveals over-scaffolding**

### Strengths
- Strong Indonesian context with real athlete names (Bambang Pamungkas, Evan Dimas, Pratama Arhan, Asnawi Mangkualam) — immediately grounded.
- Daily Wins are the most detailed of any roadmap: 11 habits across 3 categories (Pagi, Latihan, Malam). The "Small Sided Game" habit is something real footballers actually do daily.
- Small Wins are well-quantified: Passing Accuracy has explicit percentages (70%→80%→85%→90%+), Speed has measurable 30m sprint times, Strength has kg benchmarks. These feel like real coaching metrics.
- Big Wins follow the actual Indonesian pathway: SSB → Akademi → Seleksi Daerah → Debut Profesional → Timnas. The distinction between essential and optional milestones is meaningful here.
- Blueprint's "Abaikan pendidikan — sepak bola tidak selamanya" mistake is wise and realistic.

### Weaknesses
- Nutrition advice is generic ("Karbohidrat kompleks + protein + buah"). Real footballers follow periodized nutrition plans based on training load.
- No mention of social media/branding — modern footballers build personal brands (e.g. Pratama Arhan's marketability).
- Big Wins gap: No milestone for "overcoming major injury/surgery comeback" — the single biggest test for any real footballer.
- "Doa & Refleksi" as a daily habit — while culturally appropriate for Indonesia, it feels templated (appears in multiple roadmaps identically).

### Missing Elements
- Injury prevention and recovery protocols (specific exercises, physio sessions)
- Agent/manager relationship and contract negotiation education
- Video analysis habit more specific — "analisis video pertandingan sendiri" is mentioned but should be in Daily Wins
- Off-season vs in-season periodization
- Akademi dropout/alternative path (many players don't make it — what then?)

### Recommendations
- Replace generic "Sarapan Sehat" with position-specific nutrition guidance (striker vs defender vs keeper)
- Add a "Media & Personal Branding" daily habit category with habits like "Post 1 training update," "Engage with fans"
- Split the "Latihan" category into separate technical and tactical sessions
- Add a "Comeback from Injury" Big Win milestone
- Add positional specialization to Small Wins (defender skills, midfielder passing range, striker movement)

## 2. ENTREPRENEUR
**144 lines · ~936 words**

### Strengths
- Daily Wins are excellent: "Review Goal Harian (3 prioritas)", "Customer Development", "Digital Detox 1 Jam" — these are habits real founders actually do.
- Small Wins skill progression is well-structured: Product Development (Validasi→MVP→PMF→Scale) mirrors the startup lifecycle.
- Big Wins capture the real startup journey: Validasi → MVP → First Revenue → PMF → Profitable → Scale.
- "Cash flow vs revenue" awareness in common mistakes shows startup-world understanding.
- Blueprint's "Founder-market fit" and "Modal cukup untuk 18-24 bulan" are critical real-world insights.

### Weaknesses
- Over-indexes on tech startups. All success examples are tech unicorn founders (Gojek, Tokopedia, Bukalapak, Traveloka). No representation of F&B, fashion, manufacturing, agriculture, or social enterprises — which are equally valid paths.
- "Bangun Pagi (05:30)" — overused trope. Many successful founders (e.g., Patrick Collison) sleep in and work late.
- Too few skills: only 3 skills across 2 categories. Missing: hiring/culture, fundraising, legal/regulatory, product design.
- "Scale dengan funding" as "Pro" level implies funding is the goal — dangerous message. Bootstrapping is equally valid.
- Duration "1-3 tahun" is misleading — most businesses take 3-5 years to stabilize.

### Missing Elements
- Legal entity setup (PT/UMKM/CV) — a practical early milestone
- Co-founder dynamics and equity splitting
- Pricing strategy (mentioned in mistakes but not in skills)
- Unit economics, CAC/LTV — critical but absent
- Physical product vs digital product vs service business — very different paths
- Pivot moments — when to change direction

### Recommendations
- Broaden success examples to include non-tech entrepreneurs (e.g., restaurateurs, fashion brand founders, social entrepreneurs)
- Replace "Bangun Pagi (05:30)" with more founder-authentic habits like "First revenue task before noon" or "Block 90-min deep work"
- Add a "People & Culture" skill category (hiring, firing, managing)
- Add "Legal & Compliance" as a skill
- Include bootstrapping path alongside funded path
- Extend estimated journey to 3-5 years

## 3. PROGRAMMER
**152 lines · ~??? words (original seed, included as existing V3 roadmap)**

### Strengths
- Clear progression path from zero to professional.
- HTML & CSS → JS/TS → React/Next.js frontend track mirrors the actual industry hiring ladder.
- Big Wins follow real career progression: Website → Bootcamp → Open Source PR → Portfolio → First Job → Lead → Mentor.
- Blueprint is the strongest of all roadmaps: "Tutorial hell", "Impostor syndrome", "Perfect is enemy of done" — these are real struggles every developer has faced.
- "AI Assistant: Claude, Cursor, GitHub Copilot" in tools is current and honest.
- Side project emphasis is correct.

### Weaknesses
- Backend skill is too narrow: only "Node.js / Python". Missing: Go, Rust, Java/.NET for enterprise, mobile development.
- Missing DevOps/cloud skills entirely — no Docker, K8s, AWS/GCP, CI/CD.
- Missing system design — critical for senior roles.
- No testing skills in Small Wins (only in Blueprint mention).
- No data structures & algorithms practice as a Small Win (only in Blueprint). For interview prep, this needs explicit skill tracking.
- "Solve 1 Algorithm Challenge" daily — good habit but not universal. Many productive programmers don't do LeetCode daily.

### Missing Elements
- Code review as a skill (receiving AND giving feedback)
- API design best practices (RESTful conventions, GraphQL patterns)
- Database design (normalization, indexing, query optimization)
- Soft skills: technical writing, requirement gathering, estimation
- Career branching: IC vs management path
- Security fundamentals (OWASP, auth, encryption)

### Recommendations
- Expand Backend to include database skills as a separate skill
- Add "DevOps & Cloud" as a third Small Win category
- Add "System Design" skill with levels (monolith → microservices → distributed)
- Replace "Solve 1 Algorithm Challenge" with more flexible "Code for 2 hours uninterrupted"
- Add testing skill: unit → integration → e2e
- Add a "Technical Writing" or "Documentation" skill

## 4. RUNNER (Pelari Profesional)
**74 lines · ~755 words**

### Strengths
- Small Wins are measurable: Endurance (3km→10km→Half→Full Marathon), Speed (Pace 8→6→5→<4 min/km), Cadence targets (160+→170+).
- Big Wins follow real running progression: 5km Nonstop → First Race → Sub-30 5km → Half Marathon → Full Marathon → Podium → National Qualifier.
- "Dengarkan tubuhmu — istirahat sama penting dengan latihan" in mindset is crucial for runners.
- Hydration belt in tools is a detail only runners would think of.

### Weaknesses
- Too thin — 74 lines vs 180 for football player. Incomplete compared to sports peers.
- Daily Wins are generic: "Bangun Pagi (05:00)" again. Elite runners often double-session (morning + afternoon); this isn't captured.
- No cross-training specificity. "Strength Training" is vague — runners need specific: plyometrics, single-leg work, hip strength, calf raises.
- No injury prevention roadmap: shin splints, runner's knee, IT band syndrome — every runner deals with these.
- No categorization by distance: sprint vs middle-distance vs marathon vs ultra require completely different training.
- No heart rate zone training — critical for runners.

### Missing Elements
- Shoe rotation guidance (easy day shoes, race shoes, trail shoes)
- Race nutrition plan (gels, hydration strategy, carb-loading)
- Periodization (base building → speed work → taper → race → recovery)
- Stretching/mobility routine specificity
- Common running injuries and their prevention exercises
- Strength training specificity (which exercises for runners)

### Recommendations
- Add a "Recovery & Injury Prevention" skills category
- Add "Heart Rate Zone Training" as a skill with zones as levels
- Split into distance-specific sub-paths (or at least note the differences)
- Add race nutrition as a Blueprint section
- Add more Indonesian running context (events like Borobudur Marathon, Jakarta Marathon)
- Double the daily habits to reflect the dual-session training real runners do

## 5. MUSICIAN
**68 lines · ~703 words — ⚠️ Shortest roadmap overall**

### Strengths
- Daily Wins are well-balanced: Warm-up, Technique, Repertoire, Improvisation, Record Ideas, Listen to Music, Theory, Ear Training. This actually covers the full musician's day.
- "Rekam dan evaluasi permainan sendiri" in Blueprint habits is real growth advice.
- Ear training as a daily habit — often overlooked but essential.
- "Stage presence dan performance" in Blueprint skills is important.

### Weaknesses
- ⚠️ **Severely under-built.** Only 68 lines — half the length of even the other "thin" roadmaps. This roadmap does not pass quality bar.
- Small Wins are critically thin: only 2 skills, and they're generic. "Teknik Instrumen" and "Musicianship" — no instrument-specific content, no theory progression, no genre specialization.
- No distinction between different instruments: guitar vs piano vs drums vs vocal vs strings — COMPLETELY different skill trees.
- No music production/DAW skills. Modern musicians need recording, mixing, production.
- No songwriting or composition skill.
- No performance anxiety / stage fright content.
- "Andre Dinuth" (gitaris) — if user plays piano, all examples are irrelevant.
- Big Wins missing: grades/exams (ABRSM, Yamaha), competition, formal music education.

### Missing Elements
- Instrument-specific skill trees (at minimum separate guitar, piano, vocal paths)
- Music theory progressive levels (scales, modes, harmony, counterpoint)
- Sight-reading skill
- Music production (DAW, recording, mixing basics)
- Songwriting/composition skill
- Performance psychology and stagecraft
- Session musician vs solo artist vs band member — different paths
- Genre specialization (classical, jazz, pop, rock, traditional)

### Recommendations
- ⚠️ **This roadmap needs a full rewrite.** It's the weakest by a large margin.
- At minimum: add instrument categories as Small Wins categories, add music theory as a separate skill, add production skills.
- Better approach: split into instrument-specific roadmaps (gitaris, pianis, vokalis, produser musik).
- Add ABRSM/Yamaha grade progression as Big Wins.
- Add competition/award milestones.
- Add composition/songwriting as a separate skill track.

## 6. DOCTOR
**69 lines · ~685 words**

### Strengths
- Big Wins perfectly mirror the actual Indonesian medical education pipeline: Pre-Klinik → Koas (Kepaniteraan) → UKMPPD → Sumpah → Internship → Praktik Mandiri. This is *exactly* how it works.
- "Flashcard Farmakologi (review 10 obat baru)" in daily habits is what real med students actually do (Anki).
- "Me Time 30 Menit" shows awareness of the burnout crisis in medical education.
- "Teman seangkatan yang supportif" in success factors is honest — surviving med school requires peer support.
- Career paths include "Dokter Militer" — uniquely Indonesian and accurate.

### Weaknesses
- Too thin — only 69 lines for a 5-7 year journey. More detailed than musician but still sparse.
- Small Wins are critically underequipped: only 2 skills (Ilmu Dasar Kedokteran, Keterampilan Klinis). Missing: pharmacology, communication, ethics, public health, research.
- Daily Wins don't reflect the actual med student lifestyle (which is often all-day classes + self-study at night).
- No distinction between pre-klinik and koas phases — they're very different daily realities.
- No mention of research/publication — increasingly important for residency applications.
- No IPD (Internsip) specifics — what skills are gained during internship?
- No specialist track after graduation — many doctors immediately pursue spesialis.

### Missing Elements
- Pre-med preparation (biologi, kimia, fisika dasar)
- Research methodology and scientific writing
- Community medicine and public health skills
- Specific system-based knowledge (kardiovaskular, respirasi, gastrointestinal, etc.)
- Residency/specialist track options and timelines
- Medical ethics and medicolegal knowledge
- OSCE preparation as a skill

### Recommendations
- Add more Small Wins categories: farmakologi, komunikasi, etika, penelitian
- Split daily habits between pre-klinik phase and koas phase
- Add "Publikasi Ilmiah" as a milestone
- Add residency (PPDS) path after praktik mandiri
- Include SKD (Surat Tanda Registrasi) and SIP (Surat Izin Praktik) as essential milestones
- Add clinical rotation (stase) skills as sub-skills

## 10. ATHLETE (General — Atlet Profesional)
**75 lines · ~757 words**

### Strengths
- Big Wins progression is accurate: Klub/Akademi → Kompetisi → Juara Kota → Pelatda → Juara Provinsi → Pelatnas → Medali Nasional → Medali Internasional. This is the actual Indonesian sports pipeline.
- "Mental Game" as a skill is well-conceptualized (Kenali Tekanan → Atasi Kecemasan → Flow State → Mental Juara).
- "Pelatih yang tepat dan suportif" in success factors is honest.
- "Kalah adalah guru terbaik" in mindset is correct.

### Weaknesses
- ⚠️ **Fundamental design issue: "Athlete" is too broad as a single roadmap.** A swimmer, weightlifter, badminton player, gymnast, and boxer have ZERO overlapping daily habits or skills. This roadmap can only speak in generics.
- Daily Wins are nearly identical to Football Player (same structure, same times).
- Small Wins are dangerously generic: "Teknik Olahraga" with "persentase konsistensi" levels that don't correspond to any real sport's metrics.
- "Kondisi Fisik" skill uses VO2Max as the top metric — irrelevant for weightlifting or archery.
- Duration "3-10 tahun" is honest but shows the scope problem (range is too wide).

### Missing Elements
- This roadmap should not exist as a standalone. It should be a meta-framework that spawns sport-specific roadmaps.
- Anti-doping education and testing knowledge
- Competition scheduling and peaking
- Sport psychology beyond basic mental game
- Injury rehabilitation as a skill
- Nutrition periodization (off-season vs in-season vs competition)

### Recommendations
- ⚠️ **Restructure this roadmap as a template/framework rather than a standalone roadmap.** The current "athlete" roadmap is a weaker, generic version of Football Player.
- Alternatively, convert into specific roadmaps: badminton, weightlifting, swimming, basketball, etc.
- Keep the meta-roadmap but note that users should select a specific sport — and present the Big Wins (Pelatda/Pelatnas pipeline) as the framework, with sport-specific skills as customizable.
- Add a "Sport Selection" step before showing the roadmap.
- Remove and replace with sport-specific content.

### Categorization Issues

- **Entrepreneur** is too tech-focused. Add variety of business types.

### Global Recommendations (all roadmaps)

1. **Kill the "Bangun Pagi (05:00)" pattern.** Replace with authentic morning routines specific to each field.
2. **Differentiate daily voices.** A footballer's "jurnal harian" should record different things than a content creator's "jurnal harian." Make descriptions field-specific.
3. **Add field-specific failure scenarios.** Every roadmap should include: "What happens when X goes wrong?" (injury, burnout, market crash, rejection).
4. **Verify success examples.** Replace fake/barely-known names with real, recognizable figures (especially Digital Marketer and Golfer).
5. **Level up Musician and Doctor.** They are currently below acceptable content depth.
6. **Address the Athlete problem.** Either convert to a meta-framework or create sport-specific versions.
7. **Add branching paths.** Most roadmaps assume a linear progression. Real careers branch: footballer who becomes a coach, doctor who becomes a researcher, programmer who becomes a manager.
8. **Localize tools and practices.** Indonesian-specific tools/platforms for each roadmap (e.g., Bukalapak for entrepreneur, Halodoc for doctor, Gojek for business).
